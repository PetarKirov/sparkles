## Discrepancies

⚠ **D1 — wrong locator: "main disadvantage…space consumption" is §1, not Abstract.**
`docs/research/parsing/theory/peg-packrat.md:287` attributes the quote to
`[Ford 2002][packrat-icfp], Abstract`. The text is verbatim but lives in **§1
Introduction** (ford2002 txt 106-109); the Ford 2002 abstract (txt 14-28) does not
contain it. **Fix:** change `Abstract` → `§1` (or "§1, Introduction") at line 287.
(NB lines 304 and 559 cite "Abstract" correctly — those quotes really are in the
abstract.)

⚠ **D2 — unsourced quotation "globally longest match".**
`…:371`: `"there is no guarantee that this greedy matching will find the globally
longest match."` is rendered as a quotation but appears in **neither** Ford paper
(`grep -i "globally longest"` / `"greedy matching"` over both extractions → no hit),
and no citation is attached. Likely an editorial paraphrase. **Fix:** drop the quote
marks (make it the doc's own prose) or attribute to a real source. Ford's own wording
nearby is the §2.3 ordered-choice discussion (already quoted correctly at L373-374).

⚠ **D3 — Warth "bypass" quote: verb tense altered + locator off by one subsection.**
`…:481`: quote `"bypasses the memo table and re-evaluates the body of any rule
involved in the left recursion"` cited to §3.4. Source (warth2008 §3.3, txt 466-468)
reads `"…we bypass the memo table and re-evaluate the body of any rule involved in the
left recursion."` Verbs were re-conjugated (bypass→bypasses, re-evaluate→re-evaluates)
inside quotation marks, and the line sits at the end of **§3.3**, not §3.4 (the §3.4
citation is correct for the adjacent `LR`/`HEAD`/`involvedSet`/`evalSet` structures).
**Fix:** quote verbatim ("we bypass…and re-evaluate…") or de-quote; optionally note the
source line is §3.3.

⚠ **D4 — minor misquote "can yield" vs "to yield".**
`…:483`: `"can yield super-linear parse times for some left-recursive grammars"`.
Source (warth2008 Abstract txt 30; §1 txt 122-124) reads `"it is possible for a packrat
parser with our modification **to** yield super-linear parse times for some
left-recursive grammars"`. The quoted span substitutes "can" for "to" at the boundary.
**Fix:** quote "to yield …" or move "can" outside the quotation marks. (The paired
quote "this is not the case for typical uses of left recursion" is verbatim.)

⚠ **D7 — ANTLR predicate attribution overclaims `!`.**
`…:531`: "its `&`/`!` _syntactic predicates_ were Parr's contribution that Ford
adopted (§6)". Ford §6 (ford2004 txt 1152-1156) credits **only the and-predicate `&`**
to Parr ("The positive form…was introduced by Parr…for use in ANTLR"); the
not-predicate `!` "appears to be new" (i.e. Ford's own). **Fix:** restrict to "the
and-predicate `&` was Parr's contribution; the not-predicate `!` was new in Ford's
work," or reword to avoid attributing `!` to Parr.
