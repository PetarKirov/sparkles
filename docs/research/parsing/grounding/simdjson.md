## Discrepancies

- **⚠ L130 (row 26) — fabricated/misattributed quote.** The block-quoted sentence
  _"In the lookup algorithm, vectorized lookup instructions are called three times: once on
  the low nibble, once on the high nibble and once on the high nibble of the next byte, using
  three corresponding 16-byte lookup tables."_ attributed to KL21 **does not appear** in
  `keiser-lemire-2021-validating-utf8-spe.pdf` (nor in the repo). The paper's actual wording:
  "this particular application of vectorized classification uses **three different table
  lookups, instead of merely two**" (KL21:867–868) and "we create **three 16-entry lookup
  tables**" (KL21:871); the per-byte description is KL21:964/978–979 (`byte_1_high`,
  `byte_1_low`, `byte_2_high`). The _content_ is correct (matches
  `utf8_lookup4_algorithm.h:44,60,88,104`), but the quotation marks assert a verbatim source
  string that does not exist. **Fix:** drop the quotation/blockquote and paraphrase, or
  replace with a genuine quote, e.g. "uses three different table lookups, instead of merely
  two" (KL21).

- **⚠ L133 (row 30) — "~13 GB/s" misattributed to the utf8-paper.** KL21 states "We
  consistently **exceed 10 GiB s⁻¹**" (KL21:85), not 13 GB/s. The "validate UTF-8 at 13 GB/s"
  figure is the **README** headline (README.md:16). "more than 10 times" _is_ verbatim from
  the KL21 abstract (KL21:29). **Fix:** attribute the 13 GB/s to the README (or simdjson.org),
  keep "more than 10 times" on the paper; e.g. "~13 GB/s (README), more than 10× faster …
  (utf8-paper)".

- **⚠ L219 (row 56) — quote subject changed.** Page renders `both stages "mostly read and
write sequentially in memory"`; LL19 (App. C, ~line 2920) says "**All three fast parsers**
  (simdjson, RapidJSON, sajson) mostly read and write sequentially in memory." The quoted
  string is verbatim but its grammatical subject was swapped from the three parsers to
  "both stages". 1.4 GB/s and 84 MB are both correct (Table 13; caption "(84 MB)").
  **Fix (minor):** rephrase so the quote's subject isn't misrepresented, or move the quotes
  to "mostly read and write sequentially".

- **⚠ L33 (row 5) — minor misquote.** `"[do] not attempt to validate the documents"`: LL19
  has "Mison does not attempt to validate **documents**" (no "the"; LL19:180) and "…validate
  **the Twitter data**" (LL19:1280). "validate the documents" is not verbatim. **Fix:** drop
  "the", i.e. "[does] not attempt to validate documents".

- **⚠ L39 (row 8) — minor misquote.** `"…fixed cost per input byte"`: LL19:480 reads "fixed
  cost per input **bytes**" (plural). Trivial; either pluralize or mark as a silent fix.

- **⚠ L238 (row 64) — web-only provenance.** "a Rust port by the **Wayfair**/simdjson Rust
  community" and "`fast_float` … standalone library used by **Chromium, Rust**" are not
  grounded in the local README (which only lists "simdjson-rs (simd-lite): Rust port" and does
  not mention fast_float's downstreams). Both statements are accurate as general/web knowledge
  but lack a local source. **Fix:** soften or cite externally; not a factual error.

## Minor / acceptable paraphrase (not flagged for correction)

- **L218 (row 53):** page's `S = structural+pseudo-structural elements`; LL19:1853 says
  "structural and **semi-structural** elements". The paper elsewhere coins "pseudo-structural";
  treating semi-structural ≡ pseudo-structural is a reasonable gloss.
- **L166 (row 40):** "Reported … on `twitter.json` is ~3.3–3.6 GiB/s" — 3.3–3.6 is the
  **geometric mean across all Table-3 tasks** (most, but not all, derived from twitter.json),
  not a twitter.json-specific figure. 1.7× (70% faster), 1.9–2.1 DOM, and ~2.6× (find-tweet)
  all check out.
- **L116 (row 20):** "architectures without a CLMUL unit" — arm64 actually _has_ PMULL but
  deliberately uses the 6-step shift/XOR ladder for performance
  (`arm64/bitmask.h:18–30`); the framing is slightly loose but the ladder and "6-step" are
  correct.
- **L176–185 (row 45):** westmere row adds "+ PCLMULQDQ"; the doc lists only SSE4.2, though
  the westmere kernel does require CLMUL — accurate, just beyond the doc's wording.
