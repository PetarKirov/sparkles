### ⚠A — Dragon Book §4.6 misattribution + unverifiable quote (claims #4, #6-context, #11)

- **page:line** `pratt-precedence.md:184–185` (the quote), and the supporting
  citations at `:30` (refs row), `:135` ("two restrictions ([Dragon Book §4.6])"),
  `:646–647` (Sources: `§4.6 "Operator-Precedence Parsing"`). Link target
  `:688` resolves to the **2nd-edition** companion site
  (`suif.stanford.edu/dragonbook/`), and the byline at `:185`/`:646` lists
  "**M. Lam**" ⇒ 2nd edition.
- **Issue:** In the local 2nd-edition Dragon Book (`aho-2006-…`), **§4.6 is
  "Introduction to LR Parsing: Simple LR"** (`dragon.txt:475`), and the 2nd
  edition has **no Operator-Precedence Parsing section at all**. The quoted
  sentence ("Operators such as the minus sign (−) are difficult to handle…") is
  **absent** from the 2nd-edition text (no match for "difficult to handle" /
  "behave as both" near operator-precedence; the only "operator-precedence"
  string is the Floyd entry in the bibliography, `dragon.txt:14357`).
  Operator-precedence parsing, the operator-grammar definition, and this
  unary-minus remark are **1st-edition** material (Aho, Sethi & Ullman 1986,
  §4.6 "Operator-Precedence Parsing" — _no Lam_).
- **Correction (pick one):**
  1. Re-cite the **1st edition** for the operator-precedence / minus-limitation
     claims: author line "Aho, Sethi & Ullman, _Compilers_, 1st ed. (1986), §4.6
     'Operator-Precedence Parsing'", and change the `[dragon]` link used by those
     three spots to a 1st-edition reference (keep the 2nd-ed link for the §4.4
     panic-mode cite at `:456`, which is correct). **OR**
  2. Reground locally: attribute the operator-grammar definition to **Floyd 1963**
     (`floyd.txt:73–79`) + **grune-jacobs ch. 9** (local), and the unary/binary
     limitation to **Floyd himself** (`floyd.txt:1587–1600`, claim #10) — then
     either drop the verbatim Dragon quote or move it to a 1st-ed citation.
     Note: the §4.4 panic-mode citation (`:456`) needs **no** change — §4.4
     "Top-Down Parsing" with §4.4.5 panic-mode recovery is correct in the 2nd ed.
