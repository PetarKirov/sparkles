## Discrepancies

**⚠D1 — Dragon CFG quote is a misquote (concepts.md:62-65).**
Page renders:

> "Every programming language has rules that prescribe the syntactic structure of well-formed programs. … These rules can be expressed by a context-free grammar."
> Source `aho-2006-dragon-book-2nd-ed.pdf:12859`:
> "By design, every programming language has precise rules that prescribe the syntactic structure of well-formed programs."
> and (same paragraph): "The syntax of programming language constructs **can be specified by context-free grammars or BNF** (Backus-Naur Form) notation".
> Two problems: (a) first sentence drops "By design," and "precise" (alters verbatim text presented in quotes); (b) the second sentence **"These rules can be expressed by a context-free grammar." does not appear in the source** — it is a paraphrase inside quotation marks (the source says syntax "can be specified by context-free grammars or BNF notation").
> **Fix:** quote verbatim — `"By design, every programming language has precise rules that prescribe the syntactic structure of well-formed programs. … The syntax of programming language constructs can be specified by context-free grammars or BNF (Backus–Naur Form) notation."` — or paraphrase outside the quotation marks.

**⚠D2 — Dragon §3.1 quote lead-in misquoted (concepts.md:253).**
Page renders the framing sentence:

> "There are several reasons for separating the analysis phase of compiling into lexical analysis and parsing."
> Source `aho-2006-dragon-book-2nd-ed.pdf:8473`:
> "There are a number of reasons why the analysis portion of a compiler is normally separated into lexical analysis and parsing (syntax analysis) phases."
> The three numbered items ("1. Simplicity of design", "2. Compiler efficiency is improved", "3. Compiler portability is enhanced") **are** verbatim, and the bracketed `[…]` insertions are flagged as editorial. Only the lead-in is altered.
> **Fix:** replace the lead-in with the verbatim sentence above (or drop it from the quotation and keep just the verbatim numbered list).

**⚠D3 — Ford 2002 packrat abstract misquote: singular→plural (concepts.md:424-427).**
Page renders:

> "Packrat parsing is a novel technique … **Packrat parsers provide** the power and flexibility of top-down parsing with backtracking and unlimited lookahead, but nevertheless **guarantee** linear parse time."
> Source `ford-2002-packrat-parsing-icfp.pdf:14-17`:
> "Packrat parsing is a novel technique … **A packrat parser provides** the power and flexibility of top-down parsing with backtracking and unlimited lookahead, but nevertheless **guarantees** linear parse time."
> The page pluralized the subject ("A packrat parser provides … guarantees" → "Packrat parsers provide … guarantee"). Meaning unchanged but it is not verbatim.
> **Fix:** restore "A packrat parser provides … guarantees linear parse time."
