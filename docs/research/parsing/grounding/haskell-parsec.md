## Discrepancies

All are minor (locator/normalization/staleness); **no fabricated or misattributed quotes, no wrong version/date.**

- **D1 — page:47** ⚠ minor. Verbatim quote prints `(they are LL(∞))` but the source reads `(they are LL(∞) and it becomes hard…` (leijen.txt:242 — unbalanced paren in the original). The page silently inserts the closing `)`. Fix: either keep the source's `(they are LL(∞)` (faithful) or add `[)]` to mark the editorial close. Cosmetic.
- **D2 — page:182-185** ⚠ minor. The `text` block under "The result is messages like:" is a **reconstruction**, not a verbatim paper output: the paper's `run identifier "@"` yields `expecting identifier` (identifier is labeled), and `expecting letter, digit or '_'` comes from `run identifier ""` (with `unexpected end of input`). The shown combination (`unexpected "@"` + `expecting letter, digit or '_'`) is what the _unlabeled_ identifier would emit — logically valid but not a quote. Framing ("messages like") makes it acceptable; optionally annotate as illustrative.
- **D3 — page:284** ⚠ trivial locator. Quote tagged `[synopsis]` is actually attoparsec.cabal's **description** field (:16-17); the real `synopsis` (:11) is "Fast combinator parsing for bytestrings and text". Wording is verbatim. Fix label "synopsis"→"description" or accept.
- **D4 — page:283 & page:302** ⚠ trivial locator. Both quotes ("100/150 times faster"; "recover … on the fly … continue parsing") are cited `[Hackage]` but are verbatim from megaparsec **README.md** (:66-67, :70, :215-216), not the cabal/Hackage description.
