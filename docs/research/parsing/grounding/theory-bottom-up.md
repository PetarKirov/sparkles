## Discrepancies

- **D1 — `bottom-up.md:67` (Knuth handle quote page locator).** The block quote at
  lines 63–67 is cited `[Knuth 1965, p. 610]`, but its opening sentence — _"Let us define
  the handle of a tree to be the leftmost set of adjacent leaves forming a complete
  branch"_ — is on **p. 609** (knuth PDF page 3), verified by render. Only the second half
  ("This process of pruning … rightmost intermediate character at each step") is on p. 610
  (PDF 4). **Fix:** cite `pp. 609–610`. Severity: low (both pages are the primary; only the
  page number is too narrow).
  - Secondary note, `:65`: the quote renders _"corresponds exactly to [the rightmost]
    derivation in reverse"_; the source reads _"corresponds exactly to derivation (5) in
    reverse."_ The bracketed `[the rightmost]` is an editorial substitution for "(5)" and is
    factually accurate (p.609 calls (5) "the rightmost one"), and the brackets correctly
    signal insertion — acceptable, but it is a substitution, not the original wording.

- **D5 — `bottom-up.md:416–418` (Tree-sitter quote).** The phrase quoted with quotation
  marks — _ambiguity resolved "at compile-time via precedence annotations, and at run-time
  via the GLR algorithm"_ — does **not** appear verbatim anywhere in the pinned tree-sitter
  repo (`cbee4672`). The closest local text
  (`docs/src/creating-parsers/2-the-grammar-dsl.md:117`) is _"When these conflicts occur at
  runtime, Tree-sitter will use the GLR algorithm to explore all the possible
  interpretations."_ The two-tier claim (compile-time precedence + runtime GLR) is
  **supported** by the repo, but the quoted wording is a web/paraphrase artifact presented as
  a quote. **Fix:** either re-quote the actual repo sentence, or drop the quotation marks and
  attribute as a paraphrase of the tree-sitter docs.

- **D3 — `bottom-up.md:291` (conflict-naming attribution).** The text says shift/reduce and
  reduce/reduce are "named by DeRemer & Pennello and by every generator." DeRemer & Pennello
  actually use the terms **"read-reduce"** and "reduce-reduce" (deremer §1.1, txt:67), not
  "shift/reduce". "reduce/reduce" matches; "shift/reduce" is the yacc/Bison term. **Fix:**
  soften to "the two conflict kinds (DeRemer & Pennello's 'read-reduce'/'reduce-reduce'; yacc
  /Bison's shift/reduce and reduce/reduce)". Severity: low.

- **D4 — Tomita year (`bottom-up.md:99, 322, 524`).** The page dates Tomita's book _Efficient
  Parsing for Natural Language: A Fast Algorithm for Practical Systems_ (Kluwer) as **1985**.
  The local secondary (Grune & Jacobs bibliography, txt:21926) and the page's own Springer
  link (`10.1007/978-1-4757-1885-0`) both date the Kluwer book **1986**. "Tomita 1985" is
  widely used in the literature (often for his 1985 IJCAI paper / dissertation), so this is
  low-confidence, but if the citation is meant to be the _book_ the year is likely 1986.
  **Fix (optional):** verify the Kluwer book's copyright year; consider "1985/1986" or 1986.
