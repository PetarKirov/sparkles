## Discrepancies

- **⚠ D-A — Tomita "1985" (index.md:62 and :165).** Both the Foundations table
  ("Tomita 1985") and the Milestones row ("**1985** … Tomita — GLR … for natural language")
  date Tomita's GLR work to 1985. Per `theory-bottom-up.md` (D4 / register R15), the Kluwer
  book _Efficient Parsing for Natural Language_ — whose content (graph-structured stack,
  natural-language parsing) the survey describes — is dated **1986** by the only local
  secondary (Grune & Jacobs bibliography) and by the Springer DOI `10.1007/978-1-4757-1885-0`;
  "1985" is the dissertation/IJCAI date. This is **internally consistent** with the bottom-up
  deep-dive (which also says 1985), so it is a shared low-confidence date question rather than a
  cross-page contradiction. **Fix (if R15 is applied to bottom-up):** mirror it here — "Tomita
  1985/86" or 1986 — to keep the survey and its deep-dive aligned. "GNU Bison released" (1985,
  same row) is not grounded in any local artifact (plausible: Corbett's original).

- **⚠ D-C — "nom development begins" dated 2011 (index.md:172).** The milestone places nom's
  start in the **2011** column. No local artifact supports this: nom's `CHANGELOG.md` begins at
  `0.1.1 — 2015-02-06`, the `rust-nom.md` ledger records nothing before 2015, and Rust itself was
  a pre-1.0 alpha in 2011. The paired claim **"nom 1.0 (2015)"** (L173) **is correct**
  (`CHANGELOG.md`: `1.0.0 — 2015-11-16`). **Fix:** move "nom development begins" to ~2014–2015
  (or drop it and keep only the verified "nom 1.0 (2015)"), unless an external first-commit date
  can be cited as web.
